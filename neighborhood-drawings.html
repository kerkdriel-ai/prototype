Buurtkrabbels starts with one rule: the drawing IS the character. A child draws whatever they want — an animal, a monster, a dragon, a cloud with legs — photographs it, and that exact drawing moves into the neighborhood.

Design Goal No character editor. No avatar shop. Every child who picks up a crayon is already a game designer.

Draw anything

Animal, creature, monster, wizard — if a child can draw it, it can live in the neighborhood.

Gets a personality

Each drawing is assigned traits (shy, adventurous, grumpy…) that shape its needs and behavior.

Gentle care

Needs depend on personality, not species. A lazy dragon needs as little attention as a chill frog.

Shared neighborhood

Every character lives in the same world. Visit, leave gifts, make friends.

How it works
Every drawing gets a personality

When a child uploads their drawing, the game assigns a personality — a set of traits that determines how often the character needs attention and how it behaves in the world.

How personality is assigned
  1. Child uploads a photo of their drawing
  2. Names the character and describes what it is
  3. Picks 2–3 trait words (e.g. "shy", "loves food")
  4. The game bundles these into a personality type
  5. Personality sets care frequency + special behaviors
Dreamy
Often found stargazing or napping. Doesn't ask for much. Leaves little constellations around their yard.
1× / week
Wise
Slow and thoughtful. Gives other characters little scrolls of advice. Very self-sufficient.
1–2× / week
Gentle

Hides when visitors first arrive. Opens up slowly. Needs gentle, regular contact to stay happy.
2–3× / week
Adventurous
Wanders off on mini quests when left alone. Comes back with items. Needs regular check-ins.
2–3× / week
Grumpy
Hard to please, loyal once you win them over. Complains a lot but secretly loves the attention.
3× / week
Mischievous
Pranks neighbors, moves yard items around, "borrows" decorations. Gets antsy without stimulation.
3–4× / week
Playful
Needs daily interaction to burn off energy. Loves every mini-game. Bounces off the walls otherwise.

What's your character up to?
Always something fun

Glowing

Just got a visit! Bouncing around, sparkling, doing their happy dance.

Chilling

Hanging out in their yard, humming to themselves. All good.

Off exploring!

Went on a little adventure while you were gone. Came back with something weird.

Being dramatic

Putting on a WHOLE show about wanting attention. Very funny. Very extra.

Snoozing

Night time (8pm–7am). Curled up tight with little Zz bubbles floating up.

Always a happy ending

Characters never disappear or get lost. The longer you're away, the sillier and more dramatic they get — which makes coming back even more fun.

Shared neighborhood
The world keeps going

Every character lives in the same top-down 2D neighborhood. It runs 24/7 on the server — characters walk their yard, sleep at night, and react to the world even when no one is watching.

Safety & privacy
→

Your own plot

Each character gets a 4×4 tile plot. The neighborhood expands as more characters move in.

Visit neighbors

Walk to any yard, interact with the character there, leave a gift. Earn stars, make friends.

Seasons & weather

Real calendar seasons, rain, snow. Characters react — umbrellas in rain, snowmen in winter, lanterns at harvest.

Friendship badges

When two characters visit each other often, a friendship badge appears between their plots on the map.

Built-in protection
Safe by default

No chat. No purchases. No dark patterns.

These three rules are non-negotiable. All content unlocks through play. All communication uses pre-written friendly phrases. No loot boxes, no countdown timers, no pressure mechanics.

Parental controls

A parent or teacher account manages the neighborhood, approves new characters before they go live, controls all notifications, and can remove any character at any time.

Privacy first

Drawings are stored securely and never shared outside the neighborhood group. Child accounts only need a display name. COPPA and GDPR-K compliant by design.
